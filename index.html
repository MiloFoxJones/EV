Abstract

Battery electric vehicles (BEVs) have emerged as a pivotal element in the transition toward sustainable transportation. This paper evaluates the extent to which BEVs are the optimal choice for future transport needs, considering factors such as environmental impact, economic feasibility, and technological advancements. Recent studies indicate that BEVs significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions compared to traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. Additionally, the decreasing costs of battery production and advancements in charging infrastructure enhance the viability of BEVs. However, challenges such as range anxiety, battery disposal, and the environmental impact of battery production warrant a balanced perspective. This analysis concludes that while BEVs present a compelling solution for many transportation needs, a multi-faceted approach including alternative fuel technologies and improved public transport systems is essential for a holistic transition to sustainable transportation.

Introduction

The rapid advancement of technology and growing concerns over climate change have propelled battery electric vehicles (BEVs) into the spotlight. As cities evolve and the demand for cleaner transportation increases, understanding the role of BEVs is crucial for policymakers, manufacturers, and consumers alike. This paper aims to explore the optimality of BEVs in shaping the future of transportation.
