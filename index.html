Abstract

Battery electric vehicles (BEVs) represent a crucial innovation in the quest for sustainable transportation. This paper assesses the extent to which BEVs can be considered the optimal solution for future mobility. Key aspects analyzed include environmental sustainability, economic implications, and advancements in technology. Research shows that BEVs significantly lower greenhouse gas emissions compared to conventional vehicles. Furthermore, ongoing reductions in battery production costs and improvements in charging infrastructure enhance their practicality. Nonetheless, challenges such as range anxiety, battery disposal issues, and the environmental impact of battery manufacturing must be critically evaluated. The conclusion emphasizes the necessity for a multifaceted approach integrating alternative fuels and improved public transportation systems for a truly sustainable future.

Introduction

The growing urgency to combat climate change has thrust battery electric vehicles (BEVs) into the forefront of discussions about sustainable transportation. As urban environments transform and the demand for cleaner transport options rises, understanding the role of BEVs is vital for all stakeholders, including policymakers, manufacturers, and consumers. This paper aims to thoroughly investigate the advantages and challenges of BEVs in shaping the future of transportation.
